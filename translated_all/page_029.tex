\section*{Causality and Energy}

\begin{align}
    -T U_{xx} &= 0
\end{align}

where \( f, T \) are constants.

If we take \( c = E \), then

\begin{align}
    -Y U_{x} &= 0
\end{align}

Define Kinetic Energy \( E_{\text{mr}} \) as follows:

\begin{align}
    \text{KE} &= \int U \, dy \\
    &= \int U E \, dx \\
    &= \int (f U) \, dx \\
    &= p(u + u + dx) \\
    &= u + u_{xx} \, dx \\
    &= \int u + du_{x} \\
    &= T T u_{x} \\
    &= -T(U_{x} U_{x} + dX)
\end{align}
